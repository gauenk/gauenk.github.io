About Me

In these exciting times, self-driving cars are taxis, robots are chefs, and large-language models assist coders, lawyers, and medical professionals alike. These exciting applications are possible because of amazing progress in large-scale deep neural networks. However, today's large networks mehods have known limitations. They are similar to the fictional creatures of Mistwraiths (or Shoggoth), which use bones of other creatures to construct their own skeleton. These creatures (aka large-scale networks) do not care if there are three skulls instead of one, or if the feet are where the hands should be. All that matters is that ’'they work’’ according to some loss function.

Beyond the Mistwraith analogy, there are other reasons to rethink today's ‘‘data-only’’ approach to deep learning. Training with GPUs is expensive and meaningfully contributes to climate change, our responsibility to secure data privacy restricts access to important data sources, the interpretability of deep features is lacking, and current large-scale systems are untrustworthy. One approach to solve these problems is to build deep neural networks with an inductive bias. To extend our Mistwraith analogy, designing networks with good inductive biases “forces the Mistwraith to read”.

       

My current focuses uses superpixels to improve the quality of networks modules for computer vision applications. I found superpixel attention is the optimal denoiser and significantly improves image denoising quality within a simple network. I have extended this idea other superpixel-based operators (such as superpixel convolution), which also shows promising results. I am actively studying the extension of space-only supeprixels to space-time superpixels. Estimating deformable boundaries across time gives the 2D rotation matrix for free. This rotation matrix is fundamentalto many 3D computer vision tasks (ie structure-from-motion). For future work, I believe the relationship between superpixels and 3D Gaussian splatting can lead to dramatic improvement in methods to space-time computer vision.

My primary research focus is the development of new deep neural network modules by incorporating common sense prior information at the module level. The primary toolkits for my research are computer vision, probability, and Python+Pytorch coding.

These projects will take many decades of focused research across many disciplines, and I believe a core aspect of the solution will be specialized deep neural network modules derived from first principles.
