Kent W. Gauen - Biography

Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, Purdue University

My research interest is to develop reliable computer vision so that the algorithms can be used for high risk systems, e.g. autonomous systems. Real-world problems have “heavy tails” meaning many real-world example will not appear in data, even with pedabytes of data! Scientific machine learning is a framework to balance the computational efficiency of deep learning with the generalization of physically motivated models to create reliable, interpretable, and efficient simulation and inference methods. Each use case requires balancing these dimensions, and I am pretty sure I could make a career around developing algorithms and writing open-source for specific, important use cases. My moon-shot hope is that computer vision will be solved well enough for the feasible and safe commercialization of the Home Robot in the truest Jetson form.

 

Relating back to my PhD, I study reliable image correspondenace under noise. Using cameras in low-light settings is an important component of every vision-based autonomous systems. Low-light photography inherently suffers from the Poisson noise associated with photons. Wiki defines “image correspondance” as “finding the set of points in one image which can be identified as the same points in another image”. This includes optical flow and not-so-obviously unsupervised image denoising. Hence, reliable image correspondenace under heavy noise is an important for practical adaptation of autonomous systems in real-world environments.
